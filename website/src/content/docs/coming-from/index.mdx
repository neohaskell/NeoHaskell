## What this section is

- These pages are translation layers, not syntax dictionaries
- Knowing the equivalent syntax is the easy part ÔÇö the mental model shift is what takes time
- Each page covers three things: what you'd write in your language, the NeoHaskell equivalent, and what's actually different at the conceptual level
- The goal isn't to make NeoHaskell feel like your old language ÔÇö it's to make the differences legible

## The shift that matters most

- Every page in this section eventually arrives at the same insight: NeoHaskell is event-first, not state-first
- In most languages, state is the primary thing and events (if they exist) are secondary
- In NeoHaskell, events are the primary thing and state is derived from them
- This isn't a style preference ÔÇö it's enforced by the compiler
- Your existing knowledge isn't wasted. It's redirected.
